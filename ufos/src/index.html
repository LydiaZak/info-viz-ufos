Visualizing UFO Sightings

An interactive exploration of UFO Sightings in the U.S. between 1910-2014

Abby S., Emily D., Lydia Z., Peter B. - CS 7295 April, 2018

Sightings per State (1910-2018)

The first (well) known UFO sighting was in 1947. While flying his small plane, Kenneth Arnold, saw a group of nine high-speed objects near Mount Rainier in Washington. He estimated the speed of the saucer-shaped objects as several thousand miles per hour. Since then, hundreds of thousands of UFO sightings have been submitted as possible proof of extraterrestrial evidence.

The dataset that is used is from the National UFO Research Center. The dataset contains over 80,000 reports of UFO sightings over the last century, and it was geolocated and time standardized by Sigmond Axel. The data contains city, state, time, description, and duration of each sighting and gives detailed descriptions of each sighting by Peter Davenport, the Director of the National UFO Reporting Center in Washington State.

The number of reported UFO Sightings has drastically increased in the last 20 years, nearly doubling since 2001. What areas in the country are most likely to have UFO sightings? How have the sightings changed year over year? What are common shapes or descriptions? In attempt to answer these questions, we've created data visualizations using D3 in order to better understand these questions.

The map, scatter plot and pie chart shows details on UFO sightings during . Move the slider in order to update the map, plot, and pie. Hover over a dot to see the location, shape of the object, and description of the sightings. The chloropleth map is based on the number of sightings reported. Click on a state to zoom in on the sightings.
